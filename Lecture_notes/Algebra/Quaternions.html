Hamiltonian Quaternions

Definition

We defined a unital associative algebra \(\Bbb{H}\) over \(\Bbb{R}\) of 4 dimension, by the basis \({1, i, j ,k}\).
Their multiplication is defined as \(i^2 = j^2 = k^2 = -1\), \(ij = k\), \(jk = i\), \(ki = j\), \(ji = -k\), \(kj = -i\), \(ik = -j\).

The arbitrary element of \(\Bbb{H}\) is of the form \[a + bi + cj + dk \;\;\; \text{where}\;\;\; a, b, c, d \in \Bbb{R}\]

The multiplication of elements in \(\Bbb{H}\) is defined by \[\begin{align} (a + bi + cj + dk)(w + xi + yj + zk) & = (aw - bx - cy - dz) \\ & + (ax + bw + cz - dy)i \\ & + (ay + cw - bz + dx)j \\ & + (az + dw + by - cx)k \\ \end{align} \]

And the elements of \(\Bbb{H}\) is called the Hamiltonian Quaternions.

Remark

Here we give another perspective of hamiltonian quaternion.
Let \(V\) be the subspace of \(\Bbb{H}\) spanned by the basis \(\{i, j, k\}\), and let \(\Bbb{R}\) be a subspace of \(\Bbb{H}\) spanning by the basis 1.
Then every element \(h \in \Bbb{H}\) can be expressed a sum of a real scalar and and vector in \(V\), i.e., \(h = a + \vec{v}\), where \(a \in \Bbb{R}\), \(\vec{v} \in V\).
Equivalently, \(\Bbb{H}\) is a direct sum of \(\Bbb{R}\) and \(V\).

Theorem

Let \(\vec{u}, \vec{v} \in V\). Then we have \[\vec{u}\vec{v} = - \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} + \vec{u} \times \vec{v}\] where \(\cdot\) and \(\times\) is the dot and cross product in \(\Bbb{R}^3\).

Quaternion Ratation

To rotate a point \((a, b, c)\) about the unit vector \(\vec{u}\) as an axis by an angle \(\theta\).
We can define a quternion \(q = \cos \frac{\theta}{2} + \sin \frac{\theta}{2}\vec{u}\) To rotate the 3D point represented as a quternion \(\vec{v} = ai + bj + ck\), we compute \[T(\vec{v}) = q\vec{v}\bar q\]
