Group Action

Definition -- Group Action

Let \(X\) be a set and \(G\) be a group.
An action of \(G\) on \(X\) is a map \(\ast : G \times X \to X\) such that

  1. \(\ast(e, x) = x\) for all \(x \in X\), where \(e\) is the identity element of \(G\).
  2. \(\ast(g_1, \ast(g_2, x)) = \ast(g_1g_2, x)\) for all \(x \in X\) and all \(g_1, g_2 \in G\).

Under these conditions, \(X\) is referreed to as a \(G\)-set. For convenience, we write \(\ast(g, x)\) simply as \(gx\).

Proposition

Suppose that a group \(G\) acts on a set \(X\). Then for a given \(g \in G\)

  1. If \(gx_1 = gx_2\), then \(x_1 = x_2\) (injective)
  2. For all \(y \in X\), there exists \(x \in X\) such that \(gx = y\) (surjective)
Proof
  1. Suppose that \(gx_1 = gx_2\), then \(g^{-1}gx_1 = g^{-1}gx_2\), hence \(x_1 = x_2\).
  2. For all \(y \in X\), let \(x = g^{-1}y\). Then \(gx = g(g^{-1}y) = (gg^{-1})y = ey = y\).


Remark

A group action \(G\) on \(X\) can be alternatively interpreted as follows.
Let \(S_X\) be the set of permutations on \(X\). A group action \(\ast\) of \(G\) on \(X\) can be regarded as a group homomorphism \(\rho : G \to S_X\) such that \[\ast (g, x) = \rho(g)x\] This interpretation ensures that \(\rho(g)\) is a permutation for all \(g\) in \(G\) and is a homomorphism.
In other words, \(G\) acts on \(X\) \(\iff\) A group homomorphism from \(G\) to \(S_X\).

Examples
  1. Let \(G\) be a group of symmetries of an object \(X\). Then the action of \(G\) on \(X\) is a group action.
  2. Let \(X = \{ 1, 2, \ldots, n \}\). Then any subgroup of the symmetric group \(S_n\) acts on \(X\) by \(\sigma x = \sigma(x)\).
  3. Let \(X = \mathbb{R}^n\). Then the group of all invertible \(n \times n\) matrices \({\rm GL}(n, \mathbb{R})\), acts on \(X\) by \((M, v) \mapsto Mv\) for all \(M \in {\rm GL}(n, \mathbb{R})\) and \(v \in X\).
  4. Let \(G\) be a group. Then the map \((g, x) \mapsto gx\) from \(G \times G\) to \(G\) is a group action.
  5. Let \(H\) be a subgroup of \(G\) and \(G/H\) be the set of all left cosets. Then \(G\) acts on \(G/H\) by \((g, aH) \mapsto (ga)H\).
  6. Let \(X\) be a non-empty set and \(n\) be a positive integer. Then any subgroup of the symmetric group \(S_n\) acts on \(X^n\) by \(\left(\sigma, (x_1, \cdots, x_n)\right) \mapsto \left(x_{\sigma(1)}, \cdots, x_{\sigma(n)}\right)\)


Conjugation as a Group Action

Let \(G\) be a group and consider the set of all group isomorphisms of \(G\), \[Aut(G) = \{\rho : G \to G \mid \text{$\rho$ is a group iosmorphism.}\}\] For any \(g \in G\), the map \(\rho_g : G \to G\) defined by \(\rho_g(x) = gxg^{-1}\) is a group automorphism.
The map \(\rho : G \to {\rm Aut}(G)\) defined by \(g \mapsto \rho_g\) is a group homomorphism, and its kernel is equal to the center of \(G\).
As a result, the map \(\ast : G \times G \to G\) given by \(\ast(g, x) = gxg^{-1}\) is a group action.
