Automorphism Groups of Field Extension

Automorphism group

Let \(E\) be a field extension of degree \(n\) over a field \(F\). The group of automorphism on \(E/F\) is defined as follows: \[\begin{align} & Aut(E/F) \\ = & \{\rho:E \to E \mid \rho \text{ is a field isomorphism and}\; \rho(x) = x, \forall x \in F\} \\ = & \{\rho:E \to E \mid \rho \text{ is a $F$-linear field isomorphism}\} \\ \end{align}\]

Acting on root

Let \(f(x) = x^n + b_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \cdots + b_0\) be a polynomial over \(F\). Let \(\alpha\) be a root of \(f(x)\) in \(E\). Then \[\begin{align} f(\rho(\alpha)) & = \rho(\alpha)^n + b_{n-1}\rho(\alpha)^{n-1} + \cdots + b_0 \\ & = \rho(\alpha)^n + \rho(b_{n-1})\rho(\alpha)^{n-1} + \cdots + \rho(b_0) \\ & = \rho\left(\alpha^n + b_{n-1}\alpha^{n-1} + \cdots + b_0\right) \\ & = \rho(f(\alpha)) = \rho(0) = 0 \\ \end{align}\]

Therefore, \(\rho(\alpha)\) is also root of \(f(x)\).
In other words, \(Aut(E/F)\) can act on the set of roots of \(f(x)\) in \(E\).

Simple Extension

Suppose \(E = F(\alpha)\), that is \(E\) is a simple algebraic extension of \(F\).
Recall that \(1, \alpha, \cdots, \alpha^{n-1}\) can form forms a \(F\)-basis of \(E\),
and every element in \(E\) can be written as \(a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots, a_{n-1}\alpha^{n-1}\) with \(a_i \in F\).
Then \(\rho(a_0 + a_1\alpha + \cdots, a_{n-1}\alpha^{n-1}) = a_0 + a_1\rho(\alpha) + \cdots, a_{n-1}\rho(\alpha)^{n-1}\) Therefore, \(\rho\) is uniquely determined by \(\rho(\alpha)\)

Construct Automorphism

Suppose \(E = F(\alpha)\) is a finite simple extension over \(F\), and \(f(x)\) is a irreducible function of \(\alpha\) in \(F\).
Let \(\beta\) be another root of \(f(x)\) in \(E\). Then we have a sequence of \(F\)-linear filed iosmorphism: \[E = F(\alpha) \cong F[x]/\langle f(x)\rangle \cong F(\beta) = E\] which implies that \[\rho\left(\sum_{i=0}^{n-1} a_i \alpha^i \right) = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} b_i \beta^i\] defines and element in \(Aut(E/F)\)

Theorem

Let \(E = F(\alpha)\) be a simple algebraic field extension over \(F\). Suppose the irreducible polynomial \(f(x)\) of \(\alpha\) over \(F\) has \(m\) distinct roots in \(E\). Then \[|Aut(E/F)| = m \leq [E:F]\] In particular, \(|Aut(E/F)| = [E:F]\) if \(f(x)\) splits over \(F\) without repeating roots.
