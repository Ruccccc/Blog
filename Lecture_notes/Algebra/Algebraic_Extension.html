Basis for \(F(\alpha)\)

Let \(\alpha \in E\), where \(E\) is a field extesnion of \(F\).

If \(\alpha\) is algebraic over \(F\) with \({\rm deg}(\alpha, F) = n\), then \(F(\alpha)\) is a finite-dimesional vector space over \(n\), and a basis is given by \(\{1, \alpha, \cdots, \alpha^{n-1}\}\) If \(\alpha\) is not algebraic over \(F\), then \(F(\alpha)\) is an infinite-dimensional vector space over \(F\).

Algebraic extensions

Definition

An extension \(E\) of field \(F\) is an algebraic extension of \(F\) if every element in \(E\) is algebraic over \(F\).

Definition

If an extension field \(E\) of a field \(F\) is of finite dimension \(n\) as a vector space over \(F\), then \(E\) is a finite extension of degree \(n\) over \(F\). And \([E : F]\) denote the degree of \(E\) over \(F\).

Remark

The finiteness in the definition of a finite extension refers to the degree, not about the field \(E\).
For example, \(\Bbb{Q}[i]\) is a finited extension of degree \(2\) of \(\Bbb{Q}\), but it has infinitely many elements.

Theorem

A finite extension \(E\) of a field \(F\) is an algebraic extension.

Theorem

If \(E\) is a finite extension of a field \(F\), and \(K\) is a finite extension of \(E\), them \(K\) is a finite extensino of \(F\) and \([K:F] = [K:E] [E:F]\)

Corollary

Assume that \(E\) is an extension field of \(F\) and \(\alpha \in E\) is algebraic over \(F\). If \(\beta \in F(\alpha)\), then \({\rm deg}(\beta, F)\) divides \({\rm deg}(\alpha, F)\).

Corollary

Assume that \(E = F(\alpha, \beta)\) is an algebraic extension of \(F\). Let \(n = {\rm deg}(\alpha, F)\) and \(m = {\rm deg}(\beta, F)\). Then

  1. \([E : F] \leq mn\)
  2. \({\rm lcm}(m, n) \mid [E : F]\)
