Analog Signaling Techniques

image

ASK (Amplitude-shift keying)

  • Zero and constant amplitude represent 0 and 1 respectively.
  • can be used with more than two amplitude.
  • Commonly used in optical fiber.
    • One: a short pulse of light
    • Zero: absence of light
  • Disadvantage: Atteuation will effect.

FSK (Amplitude-shifting keying)

  • Using two different frequencies to represent 1 and 0.
  • Less susceptible to error than ASK.

PSK (Phase-shift keying)

  • 0: signal burst of same phase as the preceding signal burst sent.
  • 1: signal burst of opposite phase to the preceding one.

Digital data encoding

  • Receiver must know when a bit begins and ends.
  • Receiver must recognize the value of each bit.
  • Signal with greater strength will withstand attenustion and stand out from noise.
  • Higher the data rate is, the more difficult the receiver's task is
  • Encoding scheme will affect receiver performance.

Multiplexing

  • carry multiple signals on a single medium
image

FDM(Frequency-division multiplexing)

  • Different frequence represent different transmitter.
  • Transmit at same time.

TDM(Time-division multiplexing)

  • Time is divided into many time slots.
  • each time slot has unique transmitter.
  • Suppose that there are 1 link n channels. The first slot is for first channel, second slot is for second channel, ... n-th slot is for n-th channel.

CDM(Code-division multiplexing)

  • Example: In a room, there are many poeple talking in different language. And the listener will only listen to a single language, regarding other language as noise. Then, all poeple can communicate at the same time.
  • Using different coding method for different transmitter.
  • Power control is important here, since singals might effect each other.

Spread Spectrum 展頻通訊

  • Initially developed for military and intelligence requirements.
  • Spread the information signal over a wider bandwidth to make jamming and interception more difficult.
  • It's impractical to predict the sequence without algorithm and the seed.
Frequency hopping spread spectrum (FHSS)
  • Signal is broadcast over a random series of radio frequencies.
  • Hopping from frequency to frequency at fixed intervals. (IEEE 802.11 standard used a 300ms interval)
  • The sequence of channels used is dictated by pseudorandom code.
image

Direct sequence spread spectrum (DSSS)

  • Each bit in the original signal is represented by multiple bits in the transmitted signal, known as a chipping code.
  • The chipping code spreads the signal across a wider frequency band.
  • One technique: combine the digital information stream with the pseudo-random bit stream using XOR.
image

  • Infrared (IR) 紅外線

Infrared

  • unregulated 無規範
  • Diffusely reflected by light-colored objects
    • Use ceiling reflection to achieve coverage of an entire room.
  • Does not penetrate walls or opaque objects
    • Sucurity
    • Interference
  • Relatively inexpensive and simple.
  • Infrared background radiation from sunlight and indoor lighting.
Infrared Techniques
  • Directed beam
    • point to point
    • range depends on emitted power
    • Used for cross-building connection within a line of sight of each other.
  • Omnidirectional
    • A single base station (BS) within line of sight of all other stations
    • BS broadcast an omnidirectional signal
    • Other station transmit a directional beam aimed at the ceiling.
  • Diffused
    • IR transmitters focus and aim at a diffusely raflecting seiling.
    • IR radiation striking the ceiling is reradiated omnidirecionally.
