The fundamental theorem of finitel abelian groups

Theorem

Suppose that \(G\) is a finite abelian group. Then \(G\) is isomorphic to a direct product of finite cyclic groups in the form \[\mathbb{Z}_{p_{1}^{e_1}} \times \mathbb{Z}_{p_{2}^{e_2}} \times \cdots \times \mathbb{Z}_{p_{n}^{e_n}}\] where \(p_i\) are primes, not necessary distinct. The direct product is unique except for possible rearrangement of the factors.

\(p\)-torsion Subgroups

if we collect all cyclic groups in the above express with the same \(p_i = p\) to obtain a subgroup \(G_p\), called the \(p\)-torsion subgroup of \(G\), which can be also characterized by \[G_p = \{ a \in G \mid a^{p^k} = e \text{for some} k \in \mathbb{N}\] Then we can rewrite the result above as \[G = \prod_{p \Big| |G|} G_p\] where \(G_p\) is isomorphic to a group of the form \[\mathbb{Z}_{p^{r_1}} \times \cdots \times \mathbb{Z}_{p^{r_k}}\] Therefore, to determine the froup structure of \(G\), it is sufficient to determine the froup structure of its \(p\)-torsion subgroups.

Group Structure of \(\mathbb{Z}_m^\times\)

We want to study the group structure of \(\mathbb{Z}_m^\times\). First, we need to find the cardinality of the group. Recall that when \(m_1, m_2\) are coprime. The map \[rho : \mathbb{Z}_{m_1 m_2} \mapsto \mathbb{Z}_{m_1} \times \mathbb{Z}_{m_2}\] is an additive group isomorphism. Note that this map also preserves another binary structure, multiplication. Recall that \[\begin{align} \mathbb{Z}_m^\times &:= \{ \bar a \in \mathbb{Z}_m \mid \bar a \text{a has the multiplicative inverse} \} \\ &= \{ \bar a \in \mathbb{Z}_m, {\rm gcd}(a, m) = 1 \} \end{align}\] Thus, we have \(\mathbb{Z}_{m_1 m_2}^\times \cong \mathbb{Z}_{m_1}^\times \times \mathbb{Z}_{m_2}^\times\)

Cardinality of \(\mathbb{Z}_m^\times\)

Theorem

The cardinality of \(\mathbb{Z}_m^\times\) is equal to $_{p} (1 - 1/p), where \(p\) runs through all prime divisors of \(m\).

Proof

If \(m = p^k\), a prime power, we have \[\begin{align} |\mathbb{Z}_{p^k}^\times| &= #\{\bar a \in \mathbb{Z}_{p^k} \mid {\rm gcd}(a, p^k) = 1 \} &= #\{ 0 \leq i < p^k \mid {\rm gcd}(i, p^k) = 1 \} &= #\{ 0 \leq i < p^k \mid {\rm gcd}(i, p) = 1 \} &= p^{k-1}(p-1) \end{align}\] If \(m = p_1^{k_1} \cdots p_r^{k_r}\) where \(p_i\) are distinct primes and \(k_i > 0\), then \[|\mathbb{Z}_m^\times| = \prod_{i = 1}^{r} p_i^{k_i}(1 - {1 \over p_i}) = m \prod_{i = 1}^{r} (1 - {1 \over p_i})\]
