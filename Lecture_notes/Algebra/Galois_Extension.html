Zeros of irreducible Polynomials

Theorem

Let \(F\) be a field of characteristic zero. Then every irreducible polynomial \(f(x)\) has no repeated zeros in \(\bar F\).

Separable Polynomials

Definition

A polynomial \(f(x) \in F[x]\) is called separable if it has no repeated zeros in \(\bar F\).

Theroem

Let F be a field of characteristic zero. Then every irreducible polynomial \(f(x)\) is separable.

Remark

We have show that a irreducible polynomial \(f(x)\) over a field \(F\) is separable in following cases:

  • \(F\) is finite field.
  • \(F\) is of characteristic zero.

Preimitive element theorem

Theorem

Every finite extension \(E\) over a field \(F\) of characteristic zero is a simple extension.

The set of Embeddings

Definition

Suppose \(E/F\) is a finite extension and define \(Emb(E/F) =\) the set of F-embeddings of \(E\) into \(\bar F\).
It's clear that \(Aut(E/F)\) is a subset of \(Emb(E/F)\).
\(Emb(E/F)\) is only a set.

Theorem

Let \(E/F\) be a field extension, and let \(\alpha\) be a zero of $f(x) in \(E\). Then, for all \(\rho \in Emb(E/F)\), \(\rho(\alpha)\) is a zero of \(f(x)\) in \(\bar F\).

Theorem

Let \(E/F\) be a finite field extension, and let \(f(x) = Irr(F, \alpha)(x)\) in \(E\). Let \(\beta\) be a zero of \(f(x)\) in \(\bar F\). Then there exist some \(\rho \in Emb(E/F)\) such that \(\rho(\alpha) = \beta\).

Splitting Field

Definition

When \(E\) is an extension of \(F\) obtained by adjoining all zeros of a polynomial \(f(x) \in F[x]\) in \(\bar F\), the \(E\) is called the splitting field of \(f(x)\) over \(F\), denoted by \(F_f\).

Embedding Extension Theorem

Let \(E/F\) be a finite field extension and let \(K\) be an intermediate field between \(E\) and \(F\). For all \(\rho in Emb(K/F)\), there exist and extension \(\tilde\rho\) of \(\rho\) to \(E\). Moreover, if \(F\) is of characteristic zero, then there are \([E : K]\) such extension.

Separable Extension

Definition

A finie extension \(E/F\) is claeed a separable extension if \(|Emb(E/F)| = [E:F]\).

Theorem

Let \(E/F\) be a finite field extension. The following are equivalent:

  1. \(E/F\) is a separable extension.
  2. \(E\) is a separable extension.
  3. For all elemnts in \(E\), its irreducible polynomial over \(F\) is separable.

Galois Extension

Definition

If a finite extension \(E/F\) satifies:

  1. \(Aut(E/F)\) = \(Emb(E/F)\)
  2. \(|Emb(E/F)| = [E:F]\)

is called a Galois extension.
And \(Aut(E/F)\) is called the Galois group of \(E/F\), denoted by \(Gal(E/F)\).

Remark

  1. \(Aut(E/F) = Emb(E/F)\) 可以視作是\(E\)加入了\(f(x)\)的所有零點.
  2. The second condition always holds over fileds with zero characteristic.

Theorem

Let \(E/F\) be a finite Galois extesnion with Galois group \(G\). Suppose \(E = F(\alpha)\). Then \(G\) acts transitively on the set of zeros of the irreducible polynomial \(Irr(\alpha, F)(x)\) of $. Moreover \(\prod_(g \in G)(x- g(\alpha)) = Irr(\alpha, F)(x)\).

Theorem

For a finite extensino \(E/F\), the following are equivalent.

  1. E/F is Galois.
  2. E/F is normal and seaprable.
  3. E is the splitting field of a separable polynomial over \(F\).
  4. For all elements in \(E\), its irreducible polynomial over \(F\) is separable and splits over \(E\).

Corollary

Let \(E/F\) be a finite extesnion. For all intermediate field \(K\) between \(E\) and \(F\):

  1. If \(E/F\) is normal, then \(E/K\) is normal.
  2. If \(E/F\) is separable, then \(E/K\) and \(K/F\) is separable.
  3. If \(E/F\) is Galois, then \(E/K\) is Galois.
