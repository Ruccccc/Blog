Cyclic Groups

Definition

  1. A group \(G\) is cyclic if \(G = \langle a \rangle\) for some element \(a\) in \(G\). The element \(a\) is called a generator for \(G\).
  2. Let \(G\) be a group and \(a \in G\), The subgroup \(\langle a \rangle = \lbrace a^n \mid n \in \mathbb{Z} \rbrace\) is called the cyclic subgroup generated by a.
  3. If \(⟨a⟩\) has a finite number of element, then the order of \(a\) is the order of this subgroup.
  4. If \(⟨a⟩\) has infinitely many element, we say \(a\) is of infinite order.

Remark

There may be more than one generater of a cyclic group.
For example, \(\Bbb{Z}_6 = \langle \bar 1 \rangle = \langle \bar 5 \rangle\).

Theorem

Let \(G = \langle a \rangle\) be a cyclic group. If \(a^r = a^s\) for some \(r > s\). Then \(G\) contains at most \(r - s\) elements.

Theorem

Let \(G = \langle a \rangle\) be an infinite cyclic group. Then \(a^r \neq a^s\) for all integers \(r \neq s\). Moreover \(G \cong \mathbb{Z}\).

Theorem

Let \(G\) be a cyclic group of order n. Then \(G = \lbrace a^0 = e, a^1, a^2, \dots, a^{n-1} \rbrace\). Moreover \(G \cong \mathbb{Z}_n\).

Theorem

A subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic.

Corollary

The subgroups of \(\mathbb{Z}\) under addition are precisely the groups \(n\mathbb{Z}\) under addition for some \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\).

Cyclic Subgroups

Definition

Let \(G\) be a group and \(a \in G\). The subgroup \(\{a^n : n \in \Bbb{Z}\}\) is called the cyclic subgroup generated by a, and is denoted by \(\langle a \rangle\)

Theorem

Let \(G\) be a group and let \(a \in G\). Then \[H = \lbrace a^n \mid n \in \mathbb{Z}\rbrace\] is a subgroup of \(G\) and it is the smallest subgroup of \(G\) that contains \(a\). In other words, every subgroup containing \(a\) contains \(H\).

Proof

First check that \(H\) is a subgroup

  1. For all \(h_1, h_2 \in H\), we have \(h_1 = a^r, h_2 = a^s\) for some integer \(r, s\). thus \(h_1 h_2 = a^r a^s = a^{r+s} \in H\)
  2. \(e = a^0 \in H\)
  3. For all \(h \in H\), \(h = a^r \in H\) for some integer \(r\). Then \(h^{-1} = a^{-r} \in H\)

Therefore, \(H\) is a subgroup.
And it's clear that a group containing \(a\) must contains all elements of \(H\).


Problem -- Determine if a group is cyclic

Determine whether \(\mathbb{Z}^{\times}_{18}\) is cyclic.

We have \(\mathbb{Z}^{\times}_{18} = \lbrace \bar{1}, \bar{5}, \bar{7}, \overline{11}, \overline{13}, \overline{17} \rbrace\) the order of this group is \(6\).
For \(5\) \[ \begin{align*} \bar 5^0 &= \bar 1 \\ \bar 5^1 &= \bar 5 \\ \bar 5^2 &= \bar 7 \\ \bar 5^3 &= \bar 17 \\ \bar 5^4 &= \bar 13 \\ \bar 5^5 &= \bar 11 \\ \end{align*} \] Therefore, \(5\) is the generater of \(\mathbb{Z}^{\times}_{18}\).
Hence we have shown that \(\mathbb{Z}^{\times}_{18}\) is cyclic.
